# Soul Blade Patron

*Homebrew Warlock Subclass / Patron — Designed for D&D 2024 / 5.5E ruleset*

Your pact binds you to a sentient cursed, shadowed weapon, deathly patron, or hungry force that manifests through close combat. A Soul Blade is not merely a spellcaster with a sword; you are a battlefield anchor, a living curse, and a shield against enemies that would pass you by.

## Soul Blade Patron Spells

The magic of your patron ensures you always have certain spells ready. When you reach a Warlock level specified in the Soul Blade Patron Spells table, you thereafter always have the listed spells prepared.

| Warlock Level | Spells |
|---:|---|
| 3 | Shield, Wrathful Smite, Hex, Armor of Agathys, Mirror Image (testing vs Hex) |
| 5 | Conjure Barrage, Spirit Shroud |
| 7 | Shadow of Moil, Staggering Smite |
| 9 | Steel Wind Strike, Hold Monster |

### Optional unarmored variant

If the Soul Blade is intended to be an unarmored shadow-duelist rather than an armored defender, the DM may replace Wrathful Smite with Mage Armor. For the default armored defender version, use Shield and Wrathful Smite.

## Level 3: Soul Blade Training

You gain proficiency with medium armor and shields.

In addition, while wielding your Pact Weapon, if the weapon is a melee weapon, you can use one Weapon Mastery property associated with that weapon. This benefit applies only to melee attacks made with that weapon.

## Level 3: Hex Aura

As a Bonus Action, you surround yourself with a baleful aura for 1 minute. The aura is a 30-foot Emanation originating from you and moves with you. It ends early if you are Incapacitated.

When you activate the aura, choose any number of creatures you can see. The aura does not affect the chosen creatures.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

When you activate Hex Aura, you gain a number of Hex Charges equal to your Charisma Bonus. You can spend these charges on your Soul Blade features. Unspent Hex Charges vanish when the aura ends.

Unless otherwise stated, a Soul Blade feature affects only hostile creatures inside your Hex Aura.

## Level 3: Soul Reaping

When a hostile creature you can see is reduced to 0 Hit Points inside your Hex Aura, you can spend 1 Hex Charge to gain Temporary Hit Points equal to 1d6 + your Charisma modifier.

You can use this feature no more than once per turn.

## Level 3: Hexing Challenge

When you hit a hostile creature inside your Hex Aura with a melee weapon attack, you can spend 1 Hex Charge to curse its attention toward you. Until the end of your next turn, the creature has Disadvantage on attack rolls against targets other than you.

## Level 6: Hexed Steel

Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack while it is inside your Hex Aura, you can deal an extra 1d6 Necrotic damage to it.

This extra damage becomes 1d8 when you reach Warlock level 10 and 1d12 when you reach Warlock level 14.

## Level 6: Nimble Escape

While your Hex Aura is active, you can spend 1 Hex Charge as a Bonus Action. Until the end of the current turn, your movement does not provoke Opportunity Attacks from creatures inside your Hex Aura.

## Level 6: Hexbound Specter

Once per Long Rest, when a hostile creature is reduced to 0 Hit Points inside your Hex Aura, you can bind a fragment of its departing spirit. The spirit appears as a Hexbound Specter in an unoccupied space you can see inside your Hex Aura.

The Hexbound Specter is friendly to you and your allies. It shares your initiative count and acts immediately after your turn.

The Hexbound Specter obeys your verbal commands to the best of its ability. You can issue or change a command verbally on your turn, requiring no action. The command can be specific, such as “attack that creature,” or general, such as “defend me,” “guard this passage,” or “stay beside that ally.”

Once the command is completed, or if the specter has no command it can follow, it takes the Dodge action and attempts to return to you if it can see you, or to the last place it knew you were.

The Hexbound Specter remains until it drops to 0 Hit Points, until you dismiss it, until you summon another Hexbound Specter, or until you finish a Long Rest.

The Hexbound Specter cannot create other undead, cannot reduce a creature’s Hit Point maximum, and cannot regain Hit Points except as described in Hexbound Specter Healing.

### Hexbound Specter Stat Block

Medium Undead, Neutral

**Armor Class:** 12 + half your Proficiency Bonus, rounded down  
**Hit Points:** 10 + five times your Warlock level  
**Speed:** 0 ft., fly 50 ft. (hover)

**Damage Resistances:** Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Thunder; Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing  
**Damage Immunities:** Necrotic, Poison  
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Grappled, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained, Unconscious  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 10 + your Proficiency Bonus  
**Languages:** Understands the languages it knew in life and the languages you know, but cannot speak

### Traits

**Incorporeal Movement.** The specter can move through creatures and objects as if they were Difficult Terrain. It takes 1d10 Force damage if it ends its turn inside an object.

**Sunlight Sensitivity.** While in sunlight and outside your Hex Aura, the specter has Disadvantage on attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

### Actions

**Life Drain.** Melee Spell Attack: +4 plus your Charisma modifier to hit, reach 5 feet, one creature. Hit: 3d6 Necrotic damage.

The damage increases to 4d6 when you reach Warlock level 10 and 5d6 when you reach Warlock level 14.

## Hexbound Specter Healing and Incorporeal Nature

The Hexbound Specter can regain Hit Points or gain Temporary Hit Points from your Soul Blade features. It can also regain Hit Points from a spell or magical effect that can affect Undead creatures.

When a Soul Blade feature would cause you to regain Hit Points or gain Temporary Hit Points, you can cause one Hexbound Specter you summoned to receive that healing or Temporary Hit Points instead, provided the specter is inside your Hex Aura. You do not also receive the healing or Temporary Hit Points unless the feature says otherwise.

A spell or magical effect that restores Hit Points to a creature can restore Hit Points to the Hexbound Specter if the spell or effect does not exclude Undead. Touch-range healing spells, such as Cure Wounds, can affect the Hexbound Specter in this way.

Because the Hexbound Specter is incorporeal, it cannot drink potions, consume food, benefit from a healer’s kit, or benefit from salves, poultices, or other physical remedies. Non-spell features that require direct bodily contact, such as Lay on Hands, do not affect the Hexbound Specter unless the feature specifically says it can affect spirits, incorporeal creatures, or Undead.

### Mend the Bound Spirit

While your Hex Aura is active, you can spend 1 Hex Charge as a Bonus Action to restore Hit Points to one Hexbound Specter you summoned inside your Hex Aura. The specter regains Hit Points equal to 2d6 + your Charisma modifier.

The healing increases to 3d6 + your Charisma modifier when you reach Warlock level 10 and 4d6 + your Charisma modifier when you reach Warlock level 14.

## Level 10: Warding Aura

While your Hex Aura is active, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.

In addition, when a creature inside your Hex Aura hits you with an attack, you can use your Reaction and spend 1 Hex Charge to reduce the damage by 1d8 + your Charisma modifier.

## Level 14: Greater Hex

When you reach Warlock level 14, choose one of the following Greater Hex options: Tyrant of the Hex, Reaper’s Aura, or Shadow Marshal.

### Tyrant of the Hex

While your Hex Aura is active, a creature affected by your Hexing Challenge cannot willingly move farther away from you unless it first succeeds on a Wisdom saving throw against your Warlock spell save DC.

In addition, when a creature affected by your Hexing Challenge is reduced to 0 Hit Points inside your Hex Aura, you regain 1 expended Hex Charge. You can regain a Hex Charge this way no more than once per turn.

### Reaper’s Aura

While your Hex Aura is active, once per turn when you deal Hexed Steel damage to a creature inside your Hex Aura, you gain Temporary Hit Points equal to your Charisma modifier + your Proficiency Bonus.

### Shadow Marshal

When you use Hexbound Specter, you can summon two Hexbound Specters instead of one.

When you issue a verbal command to one Hexbound Specter, you can issue the same command to any Hexbound Specters you control.

No more than one Hexbound Specter can make a Life Drain attack on the same turn unless you spend 1 Hex Charge.

If you roll Initiative and have no use of Hexbound Specter remaining, you can regain one use of Hexbound Specter by expending one use of Hex Aura.
